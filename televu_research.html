CONTEXT & BACKGROUND

TeleVU Innovations Inc. is a privately-owned telecommunication corporation that connects the clinicians and the patients on a remote-basis to reduce the wait times and to offer improved medical assistance.

TeleVU Innovations Inc. offers two different products that are connected to each other.
One of them is the access to the desktop website that clinicians can use to treat patients remotely.


TeleVU Website Portal

The other product is the service in glasses which is wearable.
This machine is usually provided to the guardians or nurses who can assist the patients.


TeleVU Smart Glass application on Mobile environment

Challenge

My design team and I were asked to provide redesign of the existing product by...

  • understanding what makes them to use the product
  • understanding the pain points that the users experience while they interact with the product

    Problem Statement

    We want to understand the user experience of TeleVU Suite in-depth, especially focusing on iSee web portal, and discover some pain points during the use.

    Research Purpose

    The aim of this study included the following considerations:

  • some questions that identify difficulties that people have encountered while using the applications
  • collecting open suggestions on where and how to improvise the structure or the layout if necessary
    • ×

    The following interview study was conducted to investigate the usability of TeleVU suite (i.e. TeleVU iSee web portal, TeleVU uSee mobile application, video call session, etc.) among the people who use the TeleVU corporationâ€™s products. Prior to the beginning phase of the questionnaires, the participants were acknowledged regarding the purpose of the study which is to learn the first impression of TeleVU iSee web portal, the overall user experience of navigation and the use of provided menu of the web portal, and the overall user experience of built-in functions during the video-call sessions. The aim of this study included some questions that identify difficulties that people have encountered while using the applications and collecting open suggestions to understand on where and how to improvise the structure or the layout if necessary. Furthermore, the participants were also acknowledged that all the information that were collected during the interview will remain anonymous and confidential. As a final note, the participants were also notified that the participation was voluntary.

    Proposed Research Plan

    The current research has proposed three types of studies to investigate and understand the provided resources in-depth, to find out how the product truly interacts with the potential customers, and how the business may thrive in the industry. The two types of studies are identified as follows:

      Usability testing and user interview

      Although actual end-user testing would be more accurate, we will conduct usability testing and user interview to gather qualitative data from participants as per client's preference. The product for this project is video conferencing tool, hence, qualitative data should be sufficient.

      Competitive Analysis

      Due to limited budget and resource capacity, we plan to conduct competitive analysis in an adjacent market which is in video-conferencing industry. Tentative candidate products for the competitive research are Zoom and Google Meet.

    User Challenges We Knew

    From the research part, we have understood that the clinicians were also seeking for a chance to provide the company a bit of feedback on the company's product. If there was anything, they were always overwhelmed with the number of patients which did not leave them a moment to speak up for themselves until we have reached out to them.

    Insights

    Lessons Learned

    Login Page

    The login process needs to be simplified.
    The clinicians have pointed out that the login process needs to be simplified.

    ×

    What did we learn?

    My team and I have learnt that the login process was not as confusing to the clinicians who were interested in using the service. In fact, the client offers tutorial to the customers before the customers can practically use the service; therefore, the clinicians understand how to login.

    On the other hand, the client's customers still did not like the fact that the login was in two-steps. Basically, they felt that it was not necessary to click the "Sign in with another organization" button if they were still supposed to write their login id and password in the following screen. They said that one-step login process will do.

    Navigation Menu

    It was difficult to navigate from one menu to another for the clinicians whilst they tried to engage with the product because the product was not interactive enough.

    ×

    What did we learn?

    Fact:

    Unlike what we had assumed, the client's customers did not encounter as much difficulty in navigating through the menus due to the tutorial. On the other hand, they still admitted that it takes time to be familiar with the menu names.

    Video-Conference Session

    It was difficult to navigate from one menu to another for the clinicians whilst they tried to engage with the product because the product was not interactive enough.

    ×

    What did we learn?

    The client's customers have confirmed that the buttons need to be re-organized. Since they already knew that one layer of menu is meant to control the Desktop PC and the other layer of menu is for the mobile screen that's also displayed on the Desktop PC, they were getting used to the controls. However, they have mentioned that there needs to be some work done in organization.

    History

    We assumed that the essential information for the clinicians were missing in the history page.

    ×

    What did we learn?

    What we have learnt:

    The clinicians only wanted to be able to modify and custom-save the recorded videos that are from the previous sessions.

    The Next Step

    After the discovery, a few user personas were created with the user flow and user journey map to meet the needs and goals of the users.

    ×
    ×

    While creating the user persona and the user journey map, we needed to think about the design. We did not want to simply draw on a white board but thought of design that accommodates the following considerations:

      Point 1 I Convenience based on Frequency
  • We have reached the conclusion that the features that is most used needs to be located in a place of the web page screen where it is well-spotted.
      • Point 2 I Ease on Navigation
  • My team and I believed that it is important to remind the users regarding what they can do after they complete a task, especially if the task is a part of a series of actions.
      • Point 3 I Interactivity
  • The product needed to be interactive.

    • For example, if a clinician clicks "call" button, the product should at least notify the clinician that it is loading the video-calling session.

  • From the original history page,
    we remembered that the records simply accmulates as the videos were recorded per session.
    From this point, we have realized that there needs to be a better approach to view the recorded files instead of everything at once.

    • Why design in this way?
    ×

  • From the original video conference page,

    The client wanted different position of the menu layer in the video screen.
    In fact, it was not only the client who wanted to see the changes but also the customers who use the client's product during the treatment sessions.

    We thought that it was familiar for everyone to see icons at the bottom of the page to indicate that they are for the PC and another layer at the top of the medium-sized screen to incidate the mobile-use.

    • Why design in this way?
    ×

    Reflection

    From the user-testing phase, we were confirmed that things were working as we have intended them to be, and we were very pleased to see the outcome.

    Looking back to this project, I feel thankful that we had a gentle client, but we still had many challenges.

    One of the challenges was the research.
    It was not easy to recruit the participants as the majority were busy clinicians afterall, so we have ended up interviewing some medical students as well as the professionals.

    Another challenge was the teamwork.
    Since we were all working asynchronously, we heavily relied on Figma, Google Spreadsheet, Google Excel, and Miro altogether to keep the updates for all of us.

    The entire course-work demanded a huge amount of work in such short amount of time.
    On the other hand, I was very happy to see that a) the redesign worked out very well, b) my teammates and the client have understood the importance of the research, and c) I have learned some valuable designing tools such as Figma.

    For the Next Step

    If there is anything that I wish things were better:

  • I still feel that it was unfortunate for us to miss the chance to learn further regarding the client company's business structure.
  • I still hope that he allows some tiny changes such as modifting the titles for videos that are saved to the history section after the users are finished with recording the session for the convenience of the users, if the client chooses to work with another team of designers in the next opportunity.
